# Awesome Music Discovery

Streaming recommendations are great for everyday listening, but they often
become repetitive. This directory helps you find the right tool or community
when you want to break out of that loop.

The goal is to develop it into a community-maintained guide organized around
the clue you already have.

> [!NOTE]
> This project is an early draft. The taxonomy and entries below are examples
> meant to start a conversation. Suggestions, corrections, and disagreements
> are welcome.

---

## My recommendations have gone stale

Before trying a new tool, see what your streaming service already offers.

- [Spotify](https://spotify.com/) — Personalized playlists, radio, and
  AI-assisted discovery. **Best for:** convenient daily discovery.
  **Limitations:** poor at expanding your existing taste profile because its
  recommendations tend to reinforce established listening patterns.

- [Pandora](https://pandora.com/) — Personalized radio built around listener feedback. **Best for:** passive, station-style listening. **Limitations:** stations frequently converge on the same pool of artists, making long-term discovery feel repetitive.

- [YouTube Music](https://music.youtube.com/) — Personalized mixes, radio, and recommendations powered by YouTube's catalog. **Best for:** live performances, remixes, covers, and niche uploads. **Limitations:** recommendation quality can be inconsistent.

- [Apple Music](https://music.apple.com/) — Personalized stations combined with editorial playlists. **Best for:** balancing algorithmic and human-curated discovery. **Limitations:** fewer discovery controls than some competitors.

## I have a song or artist

Start here when you already know at least one song or artist.

- [Music-Map](https://www.music-map.com/) — Visual map of related artists. **Best for:** quickly branching from an artist. **Limitations:** often surfaces obvious or mainstream associations and provides little context for why artists are related. If you're looking for hidden gems, it can quickly run out of useful paths.

- [Last.fm](https://www.last.fm/music) — Similar artists and tracks derived from community listening data. **Best for:** exploring established musical neighborhoods. **Limitations:** excellent for mapping listening communities, but often reinforces existing listener patterns rather than uncovering unexpected discoveries.

- [Chosic](https://www.chosic.com/) — Similar songs, artists, genres, moods, and playlist tools. **Best for:** generating quick leads from a seed track. **Limitations:** results are hit-or-miss. Similarity is often driven by broad genre or metadata rather than the qualities that make a song memorable.

## I want recommendations from people

Sometimes context, nuance, or niche knowledge matters more than algorithms.
Excellent recommendations are possible, but finding them depends on asking
good questions and waiting for knowledgeable responders.

- [r/ifyoulikeblank](https://www.reddit.com/r/ifyoulikeblank/) — Recommendations based on something you already like.

- [r/musicsuggestions](https://www.reddit.com/r/musicsuggestions/) — Open-ended recommendation requests.

- [r/LetsTalkMusic](https://www.reddit.com/r/LetsTalkMusic/) — Discussion-driven music discovery and education.

## I just want to explore

Browse music without starting from a particular song or artist.

- [Bandcamp Discover](https://bandcamp.com/discover) — Explore independent music by genre, tag, location, and format. **Best for:** discovering independent artists and local scenes. **Limitations:** exceptional for independent music but weak for mainstream catalogs.

- [Rate Your Music](https://rateyourmusic.com/charts/) — Community charts, ratings, genres, and lists. **Best for:** finding acclaimed and obscure releases. **Limitations:** steep learning curve, dense interface, and ratings often reflect the tastes of dedicated music enthusiasts rather than casual listeners.

- [Album of the Year](https://www.albumoftheyear.org/) — Critic and user album rankings. **Best for:** browsing notable new and classic releases. **Limitations:** favors albums that receive critic attention, making it less useful for discovering obscure artists.

- [Hype Machine](https://hypem.com/) — Music featured across independent blogs. **Best for:** discovering blog-covered independent artists. **Limitations:** reflects the editorial tastes of participating blogs rather than the broader music landscape.

- [Discogs](https://www.discogs.com/) — Explore labels, credits, and discographies. **Best for:** following musicians, producers, and record labels. **Limitations:** fantastic for researching music you've already found, but poor as a starting point for discovering something new.

- [MusicBrainz](https://musicbrainz.org/) — Open music encyclopedia. **Best for:** exploring relationships between artists, releases, recordings, and labels. **Limitations:** designed for accurate music metadata, not helping listeners decide what to hear next.

## I want to use my listening history

Let your own listening become the starting point.

- [Last.fm](https://www.last.fm/) — Scrobbling, listening reports, and recommendations. **Best for:** long-term taste tracking across multiple services. **Limitations:** recommendations improve as your listening history grows.

- [ListenBrainz](https://listenbrainz.org/) — Open listening-history platform connected to MusicBrainz. **Best for:** open data and cross-platform listening analytics. **Limitations:** recommendations are only as useful as the listening history you provide.

## What belongs here?

An entry should help someone **discover music to listen to**.

This includes:

- Discovery tools
- Recommendation engines
- Music databases
- Editorial sources
- Human communities

This directory is curated, not exhaustive.

General music-production software, DAWs, notation software, audio libraries,
and unrelated music utilities are outside its scope. For that broader
ecosystem, see
[Awesome Music](https://github.com/noteflakes/awesome-music).
